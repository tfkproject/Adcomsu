Comparative

Comparative: The form of the adjective or adverb being used to compare two things.

  • Comparative > Most adjectives add er to compare two nouns. Some adjectives with two or more syllables use more

The comparative: using -er and more

When we use adjectives (e.g., old, important) to compare two people or things, the adjectives have special forms.
(a): We add –er to an adjective one syllable. It is followed by the word than.
Formula :
  S + tobe + Adjective + er + than + object
Examples :


  - The golf ball is smaller than the baseball.
  - The soccer ball is larger than the baseball.

  Tommy                  Bob                  Jimmy
(3 years old)      (6 years old)      (8 years old)

  - Ann is older than Halen
  - Halen is older than Tommy.
  - Tommy is younger than Helen and Ann


(b): We use more before an adjective that consists of more than one syllable. It is followed by the word than.
Formula : S + tobe + the + Adjective + than + object
Examples :
  - Health is more important than money
  - He is more handsome than me
  - This book is more expensive than that book

The use of –er or more is called the COMPARATIVE FORM

A. Forms of comparative adjectives. We can make the comparative of adjectives like this:

  1. Adjectives of one syllable, add –er:
    Big-bigger
    cheap-cheaper
    Old-older
    clean-cleaner
    Tall-taller
    large-larger
  2. Adjectives that end in –y:
    Funny-funnier
    pretty-prettier
    Happy-happier
    easy-easier
    Dirty-dirtier
  3. Adjectives with two or more syllables:
    Famous-more famous
    expensive-more expensive
    Important-more important
    interesting-more interesting
    Beautiful-more beautiful
  4. Some adjectives have an irregular comparative:
    Good-better
    bad-worse
    Far-farther/further
    much-more
    Little-less
