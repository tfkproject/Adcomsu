Superlative

Superlative: The form of the adjective or adverb being used to compare three or more things.

  • Superlative> Most adjective add est to compare three or more nouns. Some adjectives with two or more syllables use most.

The superlative: using –est and most

The superlative (-est/most) compares three or more things or people.
(a): We add -est to an adjective one syllable. It is preceded by the word the.
Formula :
  S + tobe + the- Adjective + est + object
Examples :


  - The soccer ball is the largest of all ball

  Tommy                  Bob                  Jimmy
(3 years old)      (6 years old)      (8 years old)

  - Ann is the oldest of all children


(b): We use most before an adjective that consists of more than one syllable. It is followed by the word the.
Formula : S + tobe + the most + Adjective + object
Examples :
  - He is the most handsome in this class
  - My book is the most expensive

The comparative (-er/more) compares two things or people.
The superlative (-est/most) compares three or more things or people.

(a) COMPARATIVE
     My thumb is shorter than my index finger.
(b) SUPERLATIVE
     My hand has five fingers. My thumb is the shortest (finger) of all.


A. Forms of superlative adjectives. We can make the superlative of adjectives like this:

  1. Adjectives of one syllable, add –est:
    Big-biggest
    cheap-cheapest
    Old-oldest
    clean-cleanest
    Tall-taller
    large-largest
  2. Adjectives that end in –y:
    Funny-funniest
    pretty-prettiest
    Happy-happiest
    easy-easiest
    Dirty-dirtiest
  3. Adjectives with two or more syllables:
    Famous-most famous
    expensive-most expensive
    Important-most important
    interesting-most interesting
    Beautiful-most beautiful
  4. Some adjectives have an irregular comparative:
    Good-best
    bad-worst
    Far-farthest/furthest
    much-most
    Little-least
